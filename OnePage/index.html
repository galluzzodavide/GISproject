About AirAware

An open initiative to make air quality information accessible to everyone.

AirAware provides several key features:

  • Real-time monitoring of air pollutants.
  • Analysis of historical data for trend insights.
  • Interactive maps for spatial visualization.

AirAware collects air quality data from a network of sensors and open data sources, and presents it in a user-friendly format. The platform updates data in real-time and uses charts and maps to visualize key pollutants like PM2.5, PM10, NO2, and O3.

By making this information accessible, AirAware aims to raise public awareness and empower communities to advocate for cleaner air.

Unforgiving Footprint

Understanding the long-term impact of air pollution on health, ecosystems, and future generations.

Air pollution remains a significant environmental and public health issue in Hungary, with particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) and nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) among the most critical pollutants. Major urban centers such as Budapest, Miskolc, and Debrecen frequently report PM concentrations that approach or exceed World Health Organization (WHO) guideline levels

  • Long-term health consequences for vulnerable populations.
  • Degradation of urban and natural ecosystems.
  • Increased economic burden on healthcare systems.

These pollutants contribute to respiratory and cardiovascular illnesses and significantly reduce life expectancy. Despite the availability of monitoring infrastructure and European environmental regulations, there is a persistent gap in public awareness, real-time data accessibility, and the integration of pollution trends into urban planning and policy-making. Furthermore, spatial and temporal variability in pollutant concentrations across the country complicates mitigation efforts and hinders targeted interventions.

The lack of dynamic, accessible, and geospatially detailed platforms limits the capacity of citizens, researchers, and policymakers to assess exposure risks, analyze trends, and act effectively. Therefore, a robust, web-based monitoring and visualization system for tracking the evolution of PM2.5, PM10, and NO₂ in Hungary is essential to support environmental awareness, public health, and evidence-based policy responses.
