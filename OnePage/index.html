About AirAware

An open initiative to make air quality information accessible to everyone.

AirAware provides several key features:

  • Real-time monitoring of air pollutants.
  • Analysis of historical data for trend insights.
  • Interactive maps for spatial visualization.

AirAware collects air quality data from a network of sensors and open data sources, and presents it in a user-friendly format. The platform updates data in real-time and uses charts and maps to visualize key pollutants like PM2.5, PM10, NO2, and O3.

By making this information accessible, AirAware aims to raise public awareness and empower communities to advocate for cleaner air.

Unforgiving Footprint

Understanding the long-term impact of air pollution on health, ecosystems, and future generations.

Air pollution remains a significant environmental and public health issue in Hungary, with particulate matter (PM2.5 and PM10) and nitrogen dioxide (NO₂) among the most critical pollutants. Major urban centers such as Budapest, Miskolc, and Debrecen frequently report PM concentrations that approach or exceed World Health Organization (WHO) guideline levels

These pollutants contribute to respiratory and cardiovascular illnesses and significantly reduce life expectancy. Despite the availability of monitoring infrastructure and European environmental regulations, there is a persistent gap in public awareness, real-time data accessibility, and the integration of pollution trends into urban planning and policy-making. Furthermore, spatial and temporal variability in pollutant concentrations across the country complicates mitigation efforts and hinders targeted interventions.

The lack of dynamic, accessible, and geospatially detailed platforms limits the capacity of citizens, researchers, and policymakers to assess exposure risks, analyze trends, and act effectively. Therefore, a robust, web-based monitoring and visualization system for tracking the evolution of PM2.5, PM10, and NO₂ in Hungary is essential to support environmental awareness, public health, and evidence-based policy responses.

Pollutants Explained

Get to know the pollutants monitored by AirAware and their impact on health and the environment.

PM2.5

PM2.5 refers to fine particulate matter with a diameter of 2.5 micrometers or less. These particles can penetrate deep into the lungs and even enter the bloodstream. Common sources include vehicle exhaust, industrial emissions, residential heating, and wildfires.

  • Linked to respiratory and cardiovascular diseases.
  • Reduces visibility and contributes to smog.
  • Threatens vulnerable populations such as children and the elderly.

PM10

PM10 includes all particulate matter with a diameter of 10 micrometers or less, encompassing PM2.5 and coarser particles like dust, pollen, and mold. These particles can irritate the eyes, nose, and throat and aggravate conditions such as asthma.

  • Originates from construction sites, unpaved roads, and industrial activities.
  • Can worsen lung function and trigger allergic reactions.
  • Often elevated during dry, windy conditions.

Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂)

NO₂ is a reddish-brown gas with a characteristic sharp odor, primarily produced by combustion engines and industrial processes. It plays a central role in the formation of ground-level ozone and secondary particulate matter.

  • Triggers inflammation of airways and reduces lung function.
  • Increases susceptibility to respiratory infections.
  • Contributes to the formation of acid rain and urban haze.

Organizations and Users

Who benefits from AirAware and how it empowers stakeholders.

Empowering Decision-Makers, Researchers, and Citizens

AirAware is designed to serve a wide spectrum of users, ranging from institutional actors to engaged individuals:

  • Public Authorities – Urban planning departments, environmental protection agencies, and health ministries can use the platform for informed policy-making and timely interventions.
  • Researchers and Universities – Academia can leverage the historical and real-time data for epidemiological studies, environmental monitoring, and climate impact assessments.
  • Civil Society & NGOs – Advocacy groups focused on clean air and sustainable development can use the maps and insights for public engagement and campaigns.
  • General Public – Individuals can check local air quality levels, evaluate exposure risks, and adapt their outdoor activities accordingly.

By supporting diverse use cases, AirAware contributes to greater environmental transparency and civic participation.
